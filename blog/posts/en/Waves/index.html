Waves

<p>This phenomena that I have simulated is a very complex one, therefore to wrap my head arround it I started with a lilte 1D project.</p> <p>1D usage:</p> <ul> <li>click whereever to force the level on that x position to the height of your mouse.</li> <li>release click and watch</li> <li>you can click at any moment anywhere</li> </ul> <p>[1D version](https://jaimegonzalezfabregas.github.io /htmlProyects/ondas/1d.html)</p> <p>Once I properly understood the simulation I developed a new 2D version</p> <p>2D usage:</p> <ul> <li>left click will create a dip that will evolve into a wave.</li> <li>right click will switch between shaded mode and depth mode: <ul> <li><strong>shaded</strong>: darker tiles mean that their are looking away from the light source.</li> <li><strong>depth mode</strong>: black = 0, green = valey, red = mountain.</li> </ul> </li> </ul> <p>You will be able to see that the 2d version doesnt have perfect circular waves, they have a octogonal shape to them. This is because the celular automata part. As my cells are squared you get octagons. I'm planing a fix for this in the future, but for the moment I'm happy with what I learned.</p> <p>[2D version](https://jaimegonzalezfabregas.github.io /htmlProyects/ondas/2d2d.html)</p>
